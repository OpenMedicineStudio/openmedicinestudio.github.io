02 — About the Studio

What I built,
and why

A working studio. Built in clinical register, for work that asks to be taken seriously.

I built Open Medicine Studio because I needed a place where the work I had come to believe in could be done carefully, in clinical register, without being flattened into wellness or pushed back into a category that did not fit it.

What I work with is one thing: the conditions under which a human nervous system briefly stops defending itself, expands its range, and re-enters life with more room than it had before. The intervention is a medically supervised water fast. The instrument is continuous remote monitoring with research-grade wearables. The supervision is mine. The change is yours.

03 — What is Water Fasting

Something old,
taken seriously

A clinically established intervention with decades of medical research behind it.

Therapeutic fasting under medical supervision has been studied since the 1960s. Its effects on metabolism, autonomic regulation, inflammation, and the central nervous system are well-characterised in the published literature. I did not invent any of this. I am drawing on a body of clinical evidence that already exists.

What I add is continuous remote monitoring at higher resolution than has previously been possible, conducted in your own home, under daily physician review. The fast is not new. The way I watch it is.

05 — The Benefits — Mental and Emotional

What the fast does
to the mind

Clarity. A different relationship to your own patterns.

Without the regular punctuation of meals — and without the work, the screens, the constant stimulation that ordinarily fills the gaps — you meet what daily life has been used to avoid. Some of it is clarity. Some of it is the older feeling underneath. Boredom. Restlessness. Long-postponed grief. Quiet that has not been available for a long time.

What develops here has a name: range. A wider band within which you can meet what life brings and remain in regulation. This is the second dimension of what the fast develops. The body widens. The mind widens. Both are part of why people have undertaken this kind of work, in many forms, for as long as records of such work exist.

06 — The Golden Window

Around day three,
a window opens

A measurable physiological shift, watched for in real time.

There is a moment in a five-day fast — typically around day three, as the body completes its shift into ketone metabolism — when the autonomic nervous system passes through a measurable transition. Sympathetic dominance gives way to parasympathetic regulation. The system, for a brief period, stops actively defending the patterns it has been holding, and becomes available for revision.

I call this the Autonomic Transition Window. To my knowledge, no other clinical service is currently watching for it. It is detectable because the studio's monitoring is built to watch for it. Your continuous heart rate variability, electrodermal activity, glucose, and ketones stream from the wearables on your wrist to a secure Canadian-hosted database, where my signal-detection algorithm reads the data as it arrives. When the monitoring shows the window opening, my attention turns to you.

What happens in that window is yours. I hold the conditions. The revision belongs to you.

08 — How It Works

What the five days
actually look like

Remote, monitored, supervised by me directly.

The fast takes place at home, in your own environment, on your own schedule. Preparation begins with a structured medical intake and a one-to-one consultation in which we confirm the fast is right for you and set the ground for the week.

Kit

Monitoring devices by courier

Your monitoring kit arrives by mail: an EmbracePlus wristband, a Dexcom Stelo continuous glucose monitor, a blood ketone meter, and a blood pressure cuff. Setup takes about fifteen minutes.

Fast

Water only

The fast itself is water only. Black coffee, tea, and any prescribed medications are reviewed individually at intake. Electrolyte supplementation is used when clinically indicated.

Watch

Continuous data review

Your devices stream data continuously. I review the data each day and am reachable for clinical questions throughout. The week works best with a quiet daily rhythm — rest, light walking, time in nature, minimal work, minimal screens.

Close

Refeeding and review

Refeeding begins on day six with a carefully sequenced protocol. When you are through it, we meet again and look together at what the data captured and what you experienced.

09 — What the Experience is Like

Still, supervised, and genuinely unlike anything currently available

A closely monitored clinical experience, held in the quiet of your own home.

The structure of the work is unusual: you are held without being managed. The monitoring does the reporting for you. There is no need to perform wellness, to explain how you feel, or to justify your experience. Your body's signals do that work in the background, and I am reading them.

For people who have spent a long time being responsible for others, the experience of being held this way — present in your own life, with someone else watching the data — can itself be significant. It is part of what makes the deeper work possible.

10 — Is This For You

This is designed for a specific kind of person — and you will know if that is you

Clinical eligibility, reviewed individually.

A medically supervised water fast is a real clinical intervention. I screen carefully, because the work asks something of you, and that something is most safely asked of people who arrive stable enough to undertake it.

The fast is suited to adults who are physically healthy, able to give five quiet days to an internal environment with a clearly defined monitoring protocol. The week works best with a calendar clear of work, demanding commitments, and travel.

All participants remain under the care of their own family physician throughout. My clinical role is to supervise the fast specifically; I work alongside your primary medical care.

The fast requires careful screening for:

  • Pregnancy or breastfeeding
  • Type 1 diabetes or other insulin-dependent conditions
  • Medications that require food
  • Current or recent eating disorders
  • Significant cardiac, renal, hepatic, or psychiatric conditions
  • Acute crisis

Contraindications are reviewed individually at intake. I retain the authority to recommend modification or postponement at any point, and I use it.

11 — About Me

The doctor who
went looking himself

Credentials first. Then the question that never went away.

Photo

I am Dr. Adriaan van der Wart, MBChB, a family and emergency physician practising in British Columbia. I completed my medical training in South Africa and have worked across emergency, rural, northern, Indigenous, expedition, and end-of-life medicine in Canada for over a decade.

In 2008 I joined a forty-day sailing voyage across the Atlantic as ship's doctor for three men attempting an extended fast. They were in crisis when they boarded. By the time we made landfall they had transformed in ways that the medicine I had been trained to practise could not account for. I did not know then what I was watching. The years since have been the work of finding out.

Along the way, I have done my own fasts. I have sat with silence and with plant medicines under careful guidance. I have climbed mountains. I have burned out and come back. Open Medicine Studio is what I built when I understood, finally, what I had been looking at on that boat. It is the offering I have come to believe in — held in clinical register, made possible by the careful monitoring that contemporary medicine can now do at a distance.
